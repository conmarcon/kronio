The Trapeza Cave, also known as the Kronio Cave, lies between the villages Marmaketo and Tzermiado on the Lasithi Plateau, at an altitude of approximately 860 meters.

A steep cobbled path with steps leads to the cave in about 5 minutes. The entrance is discreet, but the panoramic view of the plateau is breathtaking.

Evidence of habitation dates back to the Neolithic period. Initially used as a dwelling, the cave was later a burial and worship site.

Excavations revealed findings from the Neolithic to Byzantine periods, including gold leaves, terracotta and ivory figurines, and Egyptian scarabs from the 11th Dynasty.
